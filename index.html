Definition of Acad A - STEM

Academic A - STEM focuses on Science, Technology, Engineering, and Mathematics, with a strong emphasis on mathematics. Students do not just memorize formulas — they apply them to solve real-world problems. Whether designing structures, analyzing systems, coding software, or teaching, we develop precision and analytical thinking. This strand transforms students into problem solvers capable of navigating the fourth industrial revolution. Collaboration and technology usage are essential skills. Ultimately, STEM bridges theory and tangible creation.

About Our Strand

Role in Society

Academic A develops analytical thinkers and problem solvers. It prepares students for diverse professional careers that require precision, innovation, and critical thinking. Through mathematics and science, we learn to analyze systems, solve real-world problems, and contribute to technological and scientific advancement.
